TODAY : MEMORIES IN MANDALAS
MAKING
ABOUT THIS PROJECT
"Today" is a diary and art therapy app for grief. Users express themselves in diary entries, building a mandala as they go. The mandala and its entries can be revisited while in progress. When the user is ready, they release the mandala for sharing into a community gallery. Once released, the mandala cannot be changed, and entries cannot be edited. Entries stay private.

The mandala is the Hindu and Buddhist icon of transience. Tibetan Buddhist monks painstakingly assemble colorful sand mandalas in elaborate ceremonies that last from hours to days, only to erase the mandala and pour the sand into a stream, to pass its energies on to the world.

Animation done in Adobe After Effects CC.

Track: "On The Way" by Ferenc Hegedus.

GUERNICA 2.016
ABOUT THIS PROJECT
MAKING
EXHIBIT
PLAY GUERNICA 2.016
"Guernica 2.016" is an interactive remake of Pablo Picasso's 1937 anti-war painting. This atmospheric piece is a statement against the airstrikes of Syria. The player flies a predator drone over a dead city, figures from Picasso's original looming ahead. Distant explosions fill the wind. The drone may smash into the ruins or figures, triggering sounds of crashing or screaming. Built and programmed in Unity. 3D figures sculpted in Maya. Sound effects from free online sources.

Previously titled "Guernica 2016", Guernica 2.016 was exhibited in Brisbane Powerhouse Arts as part of Web3d 2017, and will appear in Media Art Nexus in Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. Video by June Kim.

PUSHER : THE PHILIPPINES EXTRAJUDICIAL KILLINGS GAME, AGES 8+
ABOUT THIS PROJECT
NEWS FEATURE
NEWS FEATURE
LETSPLAYPUSHER.COM
THESIS PAGE
"Pusher" is an all-ages tabletop game where players kill drug addicts, dealers, or pushers outside rule of law and dump their bodies in the street for all to see.

"Pusher" is protest art, critical design, and satire against the Philippine War on Drugs, human rights violations, extrajudicial killings, targeting of the poor, and dehumanization of addicts. Responding to political events in real time, from Summer 2016 to Spring 2017, I developed production skills including toy design, game design, systems thinking, marketing planning, material exploration, laser printing, woodcraft, web design, print design, video editing, sound editing, voice acting, vector art, rapid playtesting, rapid iteration, and product modification, to create an all-encompassing media campaign around an insane product of a culture of violence.

"Pusher" broke into Philippine news, being featured by ABS-CBN's Balitang America and culture blog Coconuts Manila. It will be showcased at the 2017 Games for Change Festival under Civics and Social Issues. The final product:

1) The game, with tokens and rules emblematic of the War on Drugs, including guns, shanties, prison, and clear "Pusher" targets resembling meth, all packaged in a child-friendly pink gun case;

2) An outrageous 90s-style parody commercial with a self-produced script and voice acting, and;

3) A farcical website containing a free PDF of the game in the vein of Cards Against Humanity and Secret Hitler.

Start them young. Bring home the War on Drugs.
