TODAY : MEMORIES IN MANDALAS
"Today" is a diary and art therapy app for grief. It aims to nurture healing and remembrance. Users express themselves in private sequential diary entries, building a mandala as they go. The in-progress mandala can be viewed to revisit and reflect on diary entries. When the user feels ready, they can release the mandala into a community gallery. Once released, the mandala cannot be changed, nor can entries be added or erased. All diary entries stay private.

The mandala is the Hindu and Buddhist icon of life, death, and renewal. It was chosen for its beauty, complexity, and symbolism of transience. Traditionally, Tibetan Buddhist monks painstakingly assemble colorful sand mandalas in elaborate ceremonies that last from hours to days, only to erase the mandala and pour the sand into a stream, to pass its energies on to the world.

Concept video animated in Adobe After Effects CC.

GUERNICA 2.016
"Guernica 2.016" is an interactive reimagining of Pablo Picasso’s 1937 anti-war painting. This atmospheric tribute is a statement against the airstrikes of Aleppo, Syria. The player flies a predator drone over a dead city. Distant explosions pepper the wind. Figures from Picasso’s original loom over the landscape. The drone may smash into the ruins or figures, triggering demolition sounds or the cries of people and animals. There is no score in this game, only the contemplation of war. Built and programmed in Unity, with 3D figures sculpted in Maya. Sound effects from free online sources.

Previously titled "Guernica 2016", Guernica 2.016 was showcased at digital art conference Web3d 2017, in Queensland University of Technology, Brisbane, and Nanyang Technological University, Singapore.

PUSHER : PHILIPPINE EXTRAJUDICIAL KILLINGS GAME, ALL AGES
Bring home the Philippine War on Drugs.

"Pusher" is an all-ages tabletop game where players kill drug addicts, drug dealers, or drug pushers and dump them in the street. It is a satirical and subversive treatise against the Philippine War on Drugs, its human rights violations, extrajudicial killings, targeting of the poor, and dehumanization of addicts.

"Pusher" is my MFA Design & Technology thesis project. The goal was to create a media campaign around an insane product of a culture of violence, made of:

1) The game, with tokens and rules emblematic of the War on Drugs, including guns, shanties, prison, and clear "Pusher" targets that resemble meth;

2) An outrageous 90s-style parody commercial with a self-produced script and voice acting, and;

3) A farcical website containing a free PDF of the game in the vein of Cards Against Humanity and Secret Hitler.

"Pusher" broke into Philippine news. It was featured by TV network ABS-CBN's Balitang America and culture blog Coconuts Manila. It was selected for showcase at the 2017 Games for Change Festival.

"Pusher" has been copyrighted and is not for sale. Bang!
