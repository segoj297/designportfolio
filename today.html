oday is a project spurred by the passing of my grandfather while I studied overseas.

Grief Observed
I originally envisioned a stand-alone artistic memorial. My instructor suggested a medium that was less self-contained and more outward-oriented, that also helped grieving people. “Grieving isn’t really for the deceased, it’s for the living,” my he reflected. Considering this, I shifted the project. It would be an app that helped people in grieving.
Writing down my thoughts was a helpful way to manage my grief, which I discovered in research also helped grieving people. I thought about a diary app. Because of the healing properties of art therapy, I decided to add an art component. I chose a mandala for the output of the app. The mandala had long been a powerful fixture in my imagination after learning about it in undergraduate art history, and now I was finally going to use it.
The farthest I got in developing the app was a prototype mandala-maker in Pixate. I ultimately illustrated the concept through a thorough motion graphics piece.
